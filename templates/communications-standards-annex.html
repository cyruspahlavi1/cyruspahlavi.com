AUTHENTICITY, COMMUNICATIONS, AND OFFICIAL RECORD
To prevent forgery and misinformation, the Office will maintain a public, timestamped archive of official statements and correspondence.
Official Channels (Recommended)
  • A single official domain controlled by the Office, with published contact emails on that domain.
  • A public 'Official Statements' page containing PDFs with reference numbers and hash values (optional).
  • Email authentication (SPF/DKIM/DMARC) to reduce impersonation.
  • A press contact and a government liaison contact, distinct from general inquiries.

Archive Standards
All official documents will be:
  • Assigned unique reference numbers before distribution
  • Archived in PDF format with consistent formatting
  • Published on the official website within 24 hours of release
  • Maintained with timestamped records for verification
  • Protected against unauthorized modification through hash verification (when technically feasible)
Security Protocols
ANTI-FORGERY MEASURES:
• Official documents originate only from verified Office channels
• All public statements archived with reference numbers
• Email authentication protocols implemented
• PDF digital signatures for diplomatic correspondence
• Regular verification of domain and email security
Verification Process for Recipients
To verify authentic Office communications:
  • Check sender domain matches official website
  • Verify reference number exists in official archive
  • Confirm document formatting matches official templates
  • Cross-reference with published statements page
  • Contact official press liaison for confirmation if needed
