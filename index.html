Biography

Heritage and Legacy

His Royal Highness Cyrus Pahlavi represents a lineage deeply connected to Iran's modern history and the ongoing struggle for constitutional democracy.

Family Legacy

His Royal Highness Cyrus Pahlavi is the son of Prince Shahram Pahlavi-Nia and Niloufar Afshar, and the grandson of Her Imperial Highness Princess Ashraf Pahlavi, the twin sister of Mohammad Reza Shah Pahlavi.

Princess Ashraf Pahlavi - "La Panth√®re Noire"

His grandmother was called "La Panth√®re Noire"‚Äîthe Black Panther‚Äîbecause she was fierce, outspoken, and unafraid of confrontation. In July 1946, as Iran faced one of the early post-war tests of its sovereignty, Princess Ashraf went to Moscow and met with Joseph Stalin at the Kremlin. She did not go as a spectator to history; she went to fight for Iran‚Äîdirectly, urgently, and with a patriot's refusal to be intimidated.

Queen Mother Tadj ol-Molouk

His great-grandmother was Queen Mother Tadj ol-Molouk‚Äîwife of Reza Shah and mother of Mohammad Reza Shah and Princess Ashraf. She became one of the first Iranian queens in the modern era to appear publicly in an official role, at a time when the place of women in society was being contested and reshaped.

Niloufar Afshar

His mother belongs to that same line of Iranian women who carried family, country, and conscience through upheaval. In exile, she has stood for dignity without spectacle‚Äîand for duty without complaint.

Historical Legacy and Modern Mission

The Pahlavi name is inseparable from Iran's modern history‚Äîits achievements, its wounds, and its unfinished work. Under Pahlavi reign, Iran built the foundations of a civil state: schools and universities expanded; national institutions were strengthened; and the law began to recognize citizens‚Äîespecially women‚Äîas full participants in the life of the nation.

Following the 1979 Iranian Revolution and the family's exile, His Royal Highness has witnessed firsthand the displacement faced by millions of Iranians. This experience of exile, combined with his heritage, has shaped his commitment to Iran's peaceful transition toward constitutional democracy.

Connection to Cyrus the Great

Across centuries and across communities, Cyrus the Great is remembered for restraint after victory‚Äîfor restoring sanctuaries and returning displaced peoples to rebuild their lives. In Jewish memory, he is honored for enabling the end of the Babylonian captivity and the return to rebuild in Jerusalem. The Cyrus Cylinder has been invoked by many as a global symbol of human dignity and fundamental human rights.

This is the Iranian standard His Royal Highness claims: strength under law; authority disciplined by conscience; faith honored without compulsion; pluralism protected by citizenship.

Public Work and Contemporary Initiatives

Political Advocacy

His public advocacy focuses on secular governance, equal citizenship, women's full equality, national unity, and a peaceful foreign policy anchored in non-aggression and law. The Office emphasizes documentation of abuses, protection of civilians, and lawful accountability‚Äîrejecting collective punishment and revenge.

Technology and Innovation

HRH Cyrus Pahlavi has supported and helped launch Pars Network (pars.network), a privacy-preserving platform intended to provide resilient communications and public-interest media infrastructure for Iranians facing censorship, intimidation, and information blackouts.

He has also supported the Pars DAO, an on-chain transparency and accountability initiative intended to publish verifiable records, open reporting, and civic proposals in a tamper-evident form.

In the private sector, he is a managing partner of Lux Fund and co-founder and president of Lux Partners Limited (Isle of Man), focusing on next-generation privacy and security technologies, including privacy-preserving computation and applied cryptography research.

Cultural and Humanitarian Initiatives

He founded CYRUS, a digital collectible initiative inspired by Cyrus the Great, intended to promote cultural memory and support civic-minded public engagement.

He also initiated MIGA Protocol, a non-governmental, humanitarian funding initiative focused on supporting the global Persian community through transparent, mission-driven assistance, prioritizing direct relief, medical support, protection for displaced families, and assistance to victims of repression.

Artistic Expression

As a visual artist, his work explores themes of exile, identity, and the enduring beauty of nature. His paintings have been featured on international platforms including Artnet, with his piece "Gardens of Exile" sold at Sotheby's auction house, representing a mature synthesis of Persian cultural traditions with contemporary artistic expression.

Personal Commitment

"I do not treat lineage as a privilege above the people. I treat it as a burden of service: to defend Iran's dignity, to be restrained by law and conscience, and to remain accountable to the public trust."

His Royal Highness positions Iran's constitutional heritage and civilizational identity as central to a democratic future: a state restrained by law, pluralism protected by citizenship, and dignity secured for every Iranian.
